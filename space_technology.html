6g communication: challenges for IoT

New research on 6g communication based IoT sensors.

As the communication standards grow, encraving newer technologies and faster connectivity, we meet newer sets of silicon friends,the quest grows even more.

As in the recently published in arxiv.org,researchers Jingzhi Hu , Hongliang Zhang , Boya Di , Kaigui Bian , and Lingyang Song in a recently conducted research by Department of Electronics, Peking University, Beijing, China, Department of Electrical Engineering, Princeton University, Princeton, NJ, USA, Department of Computer Science, Peking University, Beijing, China, envisioned that IoT(Internet of Things) lays the foundation for various important sensing applications, supporting intelligent industrial process,environmental monitoring, etc.

"In the coming 6G communications, the internet of things (IoT) serves as a key enabler to collect environmental information and is expected to achieve ubiquitous deployment. However, it is challenging for traditional IoT sensors to meet this demand because of their requirement of power supplies and frequent maintenance, which is due to their sense-thentransmit working principle. To address this challenge, we propose a meta-IoT sensing system, where the IoT sensors are based on specially designed meta-materials. The meta-IoT sensors achieve simultaneous sensing and transmission and thus require no power supplies. In order to design a meta-IoT sensing system with optimal sensing accuracy, we jointly consider the sensing and transmission of meta-IoT sensors and propose an efficient algorithm to jointly optimizes the meta-IoT structure and the sensing function at the receiver of the system. As an example, we apply the proposed system and algorithm in sensing environmental temperature and humidity levels. Simulation results show that by using the proposed algorithm, the sensing accuracy can be significantly increased.",the abstract of the paper stated.

This vision would need deploying almost 10-fold(10 million devices per square km) more than that deployed in 5g. This would also need them to be battery-free, energy-efficient and to be used without human maintanence for a longer duration. Therefore, making it a challenging for currently existing IoT sensors to satisfy the demands for 6g, as the research paper suggests.

Due the fact that the existing IoT sensors do not support simultaneous sensing and transmission, and thus need power supplies, such as lithium batteries, for modulating and transmitting sensing results. The modulation of sensing results and the antenna requirements for transmitting signals of the IoT sensors result in non-negligible power, price, and space demands for active sensors, which makes them not suitable for imperceptible mass deployment.

So in order to meet the demand for environment sensing,the paper suggests, it is necessary to develop sensors with simultaneous sensing and transmission, which means that sensing results can be carried back by wireless signals reflected on it without any modulation.

The paper thus refers to use of meta-iot sensors. The meta-IoT sensors are printed circuits on supportive substrates combined with some sensitive materials, which together work as reflectors for wireless signals. The working principle is that their reflection coefficients for wireless signals are sensitive to surrounding physical conditions. As a result, by analyzing the reflected signals from the meta-IoT sensors, the influence of the physical conditions can be recognized. Such wireless transceivers can be part of the traditional wireless sensor networks or the equipment carried by the UAVs for sensing applications, so the meta-IoT sensors can be merged into existing IoT framework.

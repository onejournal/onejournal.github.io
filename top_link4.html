NASA's MOON DUST 3D PRINTER

3D printing technology has been beneficial for various industries, reducing workload by directly printing the required, especially in space missions where its usage increases and astronauts directly rely on these. These sufficeintly advance technology only requires various input metals or materials ,to further support its credibility and increase its usage American space agency NASA is testing a 3D printer that converts moon dust/moon rocks to solid rocks. During its trip to ISS, Northrop Grumman Cygnus cargo spacecraft carried the prototype printer. NASA has been reportedly testing out the priniting systems from a company named REDWIRE to come in handy in its Artemis moon missions, in hope of using moon dust(regolith) as raw materialfor printing. The bottom line is to use readily available materials present on the moon to make the required, thus, reducing the payload and trouble and resources required to haul lots of heavy equiment from the EARTH. The prototypes have been ttaken into consideration here on Earth demonstrating the use of printer to 3d print using regolith,but using the machine in microgravity is a big step in order to consider it for Artemis moon missions. The printer works using moon simulant: i.e., a human made compound of chemically similiar msterial to moon regolith, becuase of non-readiness and rare availibilty of real samples, but the simulant must be close enough to the real sample that it can be used to test lunar material that it can be used to test the 3d printing hardware. the stimulant is used as a feedstoc, being proccesed and fed into the printer to be printed into useful parts and shapes.

Artificial skin that can sense like real one.

Researchers in China have reportedly developed an artificial skin that could, as they say, improve the performance of prosthetics by sensing injuries. The artificial skin, like real skin skin,detect damages, by using ionic signals to detect forces and change in colorin response to the bruising process, the gel is what they call as ionic organohydrogel, loaded with spirogyran which,in response to, mechanical stress,goes over from pale yellow to blue-ish purple. They are calling it as I-skin. There are many attempts that scientists from around the world have made, that can sense stimuli through electron transmission. But,due to the use of electrical conductors these are not always bio-compatible.


In testing, the skin's gel-ionic organohydrogel and spirogyran,showed significant chnages in color and electrical conductivity on stretching and compressing,which for another 2-5 hours before coming back to normal. The I-skin, that responds like skin, in terms of electrical and optical signaling,open up new opportunities for detecting damage in prosthetic devices and robotics,the researchers say.

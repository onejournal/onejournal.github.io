Hadron Collider To The Moon

Source: arxiv.org

In a paper presented by James Beacham and Frank Zimmermann,published in arxiv.org,the researchers J.Beacham and F.Zimmermann have presented a prospect of building A Circular Collider on the Moon(CCM) of approx 11000 km circumference.CCM is also proposed to reach a proton-proton center-of-mass collision energy of 14 Pev that can surpass LHC's power by 1000x with 20T dipole-magnetic feild.

J. Beacham and F. Zimmermann are also optimistic that, in a view of that a CCM could be the leading discovery in generationally adavnced(advancing 3 gen)and a potential successor to various proposed projetcs like Future Circular Collider at CERN,Super Proton-Proton Collider,and others.

Taking in account for the various natural hindrances and atmospheric adversaries on the moon, the paper stated that it will be a politically and geographically challenging to a great extent.

But will later on prove to be a ideal site for next gen scientific research and projects.

NASA, aiming to deal with a successful and sustainable establishment on moon, through Artemis program, by 2028, with a near term goal of entire surface explorationwhich coulf further optimize placements for large scale lunar establishments.
